Commercial Lock Rekeying

Restore security and key control without replacing your locks.

Maintaining control over who has access to your business is fundamental to security. However, keys can be lost, employees leave, and copies can be made without authorization. When this happens, your business is vulnerable. CCR Locksmith offers commercial lock rekeying services as a cost-effective way to restore your security immediately.

When Should You Rekey Your Business?

We recommend rekeying your commercial locks in the following situations:

  • Employee Turnover: When a key-holding employee leaves, especially if the departure was not on good terms or they failed to return their key.
  • Lost or Stolen Keys: If a master key or entry key goes missing, you must assume it could fall into the wrong hands.
  • Moving into a New Office: You never know how many keys the previous tenants or contractors still have. Start fresh with a rekey.
  • Security Breach: After a break-in or attempted break-in, rekeying ensures that old keys are useless.

How Rekeying Works

Rekeying involves changing the internal pins of the lock cylinder so that old keys no longer work, and a new key is required to operate it. This process is significantly faster and more affordable than replacing the entire lock hardware, especially for high-grade commercial locks.

Master Key Systems

Rekeying is the perfect opportunity to implement or update a Master Key System. We can configure your locks so that:

  • A Master Key opens all doors (for owners/managers).
  • Sub-Master Keys open specific departments or areas.
  • Individual Keys open only single offices or doors.

Holiday Security Tips to Keep You Safe This Season

The holidays are a wonderful time, but they can also be a peak season for break-ins and lockouts. Here are some essential tips to ensure your Christmas stays merry and secure:

üè† Residential Security During the Holidays

  • Check all locks before leaving home: Make sure all doors and windows are securely locked, especially if you're traveling to visit family.
  • Use timers for lights: Set indoor lights on timers to create the appearance that someone is home, even when you're away.
  • Don't advertise your absence: Avoid posting vacation photos on social media until you return home. Burglars monitor social media for easy targets.
  • Hide your gifts: Keep presents away from windows where they can be seen from the street.
  • Secure sliding doors: Place a rod or wooden dowel in the track to prevent forced entry.

üöó Automotive Security Tips

  • Keep spare car keys handy: Don't leave them in your coat pocket at a party ‚Äî keep them secure on your person.
  • Lock your vehicle: Even if you're just running into a store for a quick errand, always lock your car and take your keys.
  • Don't leave valuables visible: Holiday shopping bags in plain sight make your car a target. Store them in the trunk.
  • Park in well-lit areas: Especially important during evening shopping trips.

üè¢ Commercial Security During Year-End

  • Rekey locks after employee turnover: If you've had staff changes this year, now is a great time to rekey your commercial locks.
  • Inspect panic bars and exit devices: Ensure all emergency exits are functioning properly before the New Year.
  • Update access control systems: Review who has access to your building and revoke access for former employees.
  • Secure cash and valuables: Use a commercial-grade safe and ensure it's properly bolted down.

Commercial Door Closer Installation & Repair in Metro Detroit

Surface, concealed & floor-spring closers installed, adjusted & repaired — ADA & fire-code compliant.

Properly set closers keep doors safe, quiet, and secure. We install Grade 1, fire-rated models and tune backcheck, sweep, and latch speeds so doors close smoothly and latch every time.

What We Service

  • Surface-mounted closers: standard, top-jamb, parallel arm
  • Concealed overhead closers & transom units
  • Floor-spring closers (glass & heavy doors)
  • Grade 1 heavy-duty & fire-rated closers
  • Hold-open vs. non-hold-open arms (per code)
  • Delayed action, backcheck & adjustable power models

We supply leading brands and match closer size/handing to door width, weight, usage, and rating.

Install & Repair Process

  1. Survey & Quote: Verify door/frame type, rating, swing, and ADA requirements; provide an upfront estimate.
  2. Mount & Set: Install or re-anchor plates/arms; set power; adjust backcheck, sweep, and latch.
  3. Code Check: Configure hold-open/non-hold-open per fire door rules; coordinate with panic bars and strikes.
  4. Seasonal Tune: Account for temperature swings common in Metro Detroit to prevent slamming or soft-close failures.
  5. Test & Warranty: Cycle-test for smooth closing and positive latch; back by a 5-Year Warranty.

Common Issues We Fix

  • Door slams, won’t latch, or drifts open
  • Oil leakage / failed seals → replace closer body
  • Bent/broken arms, loose shoe or mounting plate
  • Mis-sized closer for door weight/traffic
  • Improper speeds: backcheck too weak/harsh, sweep too fast, latch too slow

If the closer leaks oil or the spindle is worn, replacement is the right fix; otherwise, precise adjustment and re-anchoring often restore performance.

Compliance & Best Practice

  • ADA: Set opening forces and closing time for accessible operation.
  • Fire-Rated Doors: Use listed non-hold-open arms unless tied to compliant hold-open releases.
  • Hardware Coordination: Ensure reliable latching with panic/exit devices, electric strikes, and access control.
  • Maintenance: Seasonal checks extend closer and hinge life, preventing costly door/frame damage.

FAQs

Which door closer types do you install and repair?

Surface-mounted (standard/top-jamb/parallel arm), concealed overhead, and floor-spring closers. We supply Grade 1 heavy-duty and fire-rated models with hold-open or non-hold-open arms.

Can you stop a door from slamming or not latching?

Yes. We correctly set backcheck, sweep, and latch speeds, re-anchor loose hardware, and replace leaking units so doors close smoothly and latch every time.

Do you set closers to ADA and fire code?

Yes. We configure opening force and timing for accessibility and ensure proper hardware on fire-rated doors, coordinating with panic bars, strikes, and alarms.

Why does my door need seasonal adjustment?

Metro Detroit’s temperature swings affect hydraulic closer behavior and door/frame movement. A seasonal tune keeps performance consistent.

Repair or replace—what’s smarter?

Oil leaks or worn spindles = replace. For misadjustment, bent arms, or loose mounts, repair and re-anchoring typically solve the issue.

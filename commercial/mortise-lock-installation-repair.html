Mortise Lock Installation & Repair in Metro Detroit

Commercial-grade mortise locksets installed, serviced, and rekeyed — storefronts, offices, schools & facilities.

Mortise locks are the workhorses of commercial doors. We install and repair Grade 1 bodies, trims, and cylinders, including narrow-stile storefront hardware. Clean door prep, proper templating, and code-compliant hardware — backed by our 5-Year Warranty.

What We Install & Repair

  • Grade 1 mortise lock bodies, levers/knobs, roses/escutcheons
  • Narrow-stile storefront deadlatches & deadlocks (Adams Rite style)
  • Functions: Entry, Office, Storeroom, Classroom, Passage, Privacy
  • Repairs: springs, spindles, followers, latches, through-bolts, trim
  • Rekey/recylinder (standard, high-security, or IC cores), key control
  • Strike alignment, door prep, patch/armor plates where needed
  • Electrified mortise options for access control integrations

We service Schlage L-Series, Corbin Russwin ML2000, SARGENT 8200, Yale 8800, and storefront systems like Adams Rite — and more.

Installation & Repair Process

  1. Site Survey & Spec: Door material, backset, function, fire/egress codes.
  2. Quote: Hardware options (Grade 1), finish, trim, cylinders & keys.
  3. Prep & Install: Template-accurate mortise, clean cuts, secure mounting.
  4. Rekey & Integrate: Tie into master key plan or set new restricted keys.
  5. Test & Warranty: Latch, clearance, closer timing, and hand-off with a 5-Year Warranty.

Where Mortise Locks Make Sense

  • Glass/aluminum storefronts, office corridors, classrooms, multi-tenant entries
  • High-traffic doors needing robust, serviceable lock bodies
  • Master-keyed facilities with controlled key issuance
  • Access control projects (electrified mortise, compatible strikes)
  • Code-compliant egress and fire-rated openings (coordination with closers & exit devices)

FAQs

Mortise vs. cylindrical — what's the difference?

A mortise lock sits in a rectangular pocket inside the door and is typically stronger and more serviceable. Cylindrical locks mount through a round bore and are common on lighter-duty openings.

Can you match existing keys or add this door to our master system?

Yes. We can rekey or recore cylinders (standard or IC) to match your plan, set controlled keys, and provide key charts on request.

Do you service storefront (narrow-stile) locks?

Yes. We service and replace narrow-stile deadlatches and deadlocks (Adams Rite style) including trims, cylinders, and strikes.

How long does installation take?

Retrofits are faster. First-time installs require precise door prep; most single-door jobs finish in one visit with minimal downtime.

Do you provide high-security or restricted key options?

Yes—on request we can specify high-security cylinders with restricted key control for sensitive areas.

Will this meet code?

We specify compatible hardware for egress/fire-rated openings and coordinate with door closers, exit devices, and strikes to maintain compliance.

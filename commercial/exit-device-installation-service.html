Exit Device Installation for Businesses in Metro Detroit

Rim, SVR, CVR & mortise panic devices installed to code — fast, clean, and compliant.

We supply and install commercial-grade exit hardware (panic/crash bars and fire exit devices) on storefronts, offices, restaurants, schools, warehouses, places of worship, and multi-tenant buildings. Our technicians ensure smooth egress, proper latch/strike alignment, correct door prep and through-bolting, and AHJ-compliant setup with exterior trims as needed.

What We Install

  • Rim exit devices (standard on single outswing doors)
  • Surface & concealed vertical rod (SVR/CVR) for pairs
  • Mortise exit devices & classroom/office functions
  • Alarmed & delayed egress bars (with local code coordination)
  • Dogging options (hex/key), quiet latching, weatherized models
  • Exterior trims: nightlatch, lever, rim cylinder, escutcheon
  • Compatible strikes, latch guards, coordinators & door plates

We work on hollow metal, solid core wood, and aluminum storefront doors (narrow stile options available).

Install & Code-Compliance Process

  1. Site Check & Quote: Door swing, stile width, fire label, use-case, and AHJ considerations.
  2. Prep & Reinforce: Accurate templating, through-bolts, proper backset/stile drilling.
  3. Mount & Align: Device body, rods (if any), strike alignment, quiet/secure latching.
  4. Trim & Options: Exterior keyed access, dogging, alarms, or delayed egress where permitted.
  5. Test & Train: Function test, staff demo, maintenance tips — backed by a 5-Year Warranty.

FAQs

What’s the difference between rim, SVR/CVR, and mortise exit devices?

Rim devices mount to the door surface and latch to a rim strike. SVR/CVR use vertical rods that latch at the header/sill for pairs of doors. Mortise devices engage a mortise lock body inside the door for a clean look and heavy-duty performance.

Can I add exterior keyed access?

Yes. We install keyed trims (nightlatch/lever) so staff can enter from outside while keeping free egress from inside.

Do you handle alarmed or delayed egress hardware?

Yes. We install alarmed bars and delayed egress units where permitted, and we coordinate setup to satisfy the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ).

Do you warranty the work?

Yes — parts and labor carry our 5-Year Warranty. If anything goes out of adjustment, call us and we’ll make it right.

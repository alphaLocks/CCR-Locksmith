Commercial Lock Rekeying in Metro Detroit

Secure your business fast—old keys stop working, new keys issued. Same-day mobile service.

When staff changes, keys go missing, or a new lease starts, rekeying is the fastest, most cost-effective way to restore key control without replacing hardware. We rekey storefronts, offices, and warehouses across Metro Detroit—often the same day.

When to Rekey

  • Employee turnover or vendor key not returned
  • Lost/stolen keys or uncertain key control
  • New lease or ownership—reset access day one
  • After a break-in or attempted entry
  • Consolidate keys (keyed-alike) for convenience

Rekeying changes a lock’s internal pins so old keys no longer work—no hardware removal required.

Our Rekeying Process

  1. Verify & Plan: Confirm authorization, walk the site, map door list & cylinders.
  2. Pinning & Keying: Change pin stacks (or IC cores) to new key codes.
  3. Master Keying: Build hierarchies (master/sub-masters/change keys) on request.
  4. Cut & Issue Keys: Provide labeled key sets, optional restricted keyways.
  5. Test & Document: Function test every door; deliver a keying chart if needed.

Facilities & Hardware We Service

  • Offices, retail, restaurants, warehouses, multi-tenant buildings
  • Cylindrical & mortise locks, storefront (Adams Rite), rim & mortise cylinders
  • IC cores (Best/Small-Format & Large-Format), padlocks, cabinets & desks
  • Brands: Schlage, Corbin Russwin, Sargent, Yale, BEST, Medeco, Mul-T-Lock, and more

FAQs

Is rekeying better than replacing locks?

For most scenarios, yes—rekeying is faster and more affordable while restoring full key control. We replace hardware if it’s worn, damaged, or you’re upgrading security.

Can we get one key for multiple doors?

Yes. We can key-alike compatible locks or design a master system so staff keep one key for all authorized doors.

Can you rekey storefront and glass-door hardware?

Absolutely. We service Adams Rite style storefront locks, mortise cylinders, and rim cylinders, including IC core systems.

Do you track key records?

On request, we provide a keying chart and label issued keys for simple, auditable key control.

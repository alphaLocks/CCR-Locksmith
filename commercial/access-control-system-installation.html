Access Control System Installation in Metro Detroit

Keycard, keypad, fob, biometric — single door to multi-door networks, designed, installed, and supported.

We build end-to-end electronic access solutions: readers and controllers, electric strikes or maglocks, credential software, and clean wiring. Control who enters, when they can enter, and keep an audit trail — without rekeying every time staff changes.

What We Install

  • Card & proximity readers (badges, fobs, mobile credentials)
  • Keypad/PIN locks & standalone electronic locks
  • Biometric readers (fingerprint/face) where appropriate
  • Electric strikes, electrified latch hardware, and maglocks
  • Door controllers, power supplies, request-to-exit & door contacts
  • On-prem or cloud-managed software for users, schedules & logs

We match hardware to door type, code requirements, and budget.

Design & Install Process

  1. Site Assessment & Quote: Walkthrough, door count, wiring paths, and code/egress review with an upfront estimate.
  2. System Design: Specify readers, locks (strike vs. maglock), controllers, and software. Plan schedules, holidays, and groups.
  3. Professional Install: Clean cabling, power management, controller setup, and hardware mounting/alignments.
  4. Software & Training: Create admin accounts, import users, issue cards/fobs/PINs, and train your team.
  5. Test & Warranty: End-to-end testing with documentation and our 5-Year Warranty on parts & labor.

Systems & Use Cases

  • Small offices & retail: Oneâ€“two doors with keypad or card/fob readers and simple schedules.
  • Multi-door facilities: Networked controllers with time zones, holidays, and audit logs.
  • Apartments & multi-tenant: Readers + intercom/buzzer tied to electric strikes for lobby doors.
  • Server rooms & restricted areas: Biometric/PIN + logging and camera integration.
  • Cloud management: Manage users remotely, unlock doors from your phone, and export reports.

We integrate with alarms and many VMS/CCTV platforms and can phase your upgrade over time.

FAQs

Which locking method is right: electric strike or maglock?

For most latchbolt doors, an electric strike is preferred so the hardware still functions mechanically during egress. Maglocks are used when the door/frame canâ€™t accept an electrified latch, or for specialty glass/metal doors. We advise based on door type and code.

Can you reuse my existing door hardware?

Often yes. We can retrofit readers and controllers, add an electric strike to existing frames, or swap in an electrified lever/mortise body to keep your look consistent.

Do you offer cloud management and mobile credentials?

Yes. Many platforms support cloud dashboards, mobile unlock, and phone-as-badge credentials. Weâ€™ll review options and pricing during the site assessment.

What about code compliance and fire exits?

We follow life-safety and egress rules, specify proper REX and door contacts, and coordinate with your alarm company when needed to ensure compliant operation.

What is the typical timeline?

Small systems (1â€“2 doors) are commonly completed in a single visit after design approval. Multi-door networks are phased to minimize downtime and coordinate with your schedule.
